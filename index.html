醉生梦死，虚度光阴。是走马灯吗？是梦吗？还是现实？银色薄膜被涂抹，闪耀夺目的屏幕等间距环形排列并旋转着。声音、气味、触碰都无法感受到的无机质场所，却有光流传递着心跳的细微震动。是在现实或人生中终于脱离了吗？我一边穿过幕布的间隙一边思考。或许是遭遇了强迫性幻觉，自我被收纳于此，试图逃向非现实？在如此离奇的情况下，我仍觉得自己保持着冷静的判断。耀眼且白色的屏幕，点阵缺失却仍投影出影像，里面除了我再无他人：自愿被钉在十字架上的人类，从浓雾中现身的毛发浓密的双足动物，身着黑色铠甲的青年站在浅滩，穿着白色大衣蓄着唇髭的老人沉思后朝我看了一眼，闭目禅坐的僧侣身后有赫赫金色的水纹。還有一些难以理解或毫无脉络的投影。无声、断续、画质低劣，与银幕相对时，黑暗沉重的某种东西如镜子般显现，像是无边无际的寒冷安置室，永远映照着。我感到背脊发凉，发现一幅屏幕上投影出乳白色夹杂负面色彩的画作。
卡！导演露出享受恐惧情绪和负面风险的表情，悄悄对着耳朵深处的小指第一关节大小的925金属银色对讲机低语。很好，演技很棒！动作和细节都很到位，果然没有你这部电影无法想象。今天拍到了很棒的画面，谢谢你！明天也拜托了。日落前，带着积极与消极交织的气息，位于东侧砖墙风格的废弃仓库“达罗”。这位导演，白发夹杂，戴着可疑的锈迹斑斑的金边八角形太阳镜，与我交往大概有十年了吧？每次见面，他都散发着浅薄的野性魅力，涂着仿佛宣称只对勾引女性感兴趣的香水。应该是用了不错的佛手柑和木质调，但比例不对，异戊酸异戊酯太过浓烈。你从哪儿弄来这么复古的香水？几十年前的东西了吧？听说你有存货，但能找到保存这么好的可不容易。我其实并不在意，但出于沟通和纯粹好奇问了一句。
劳动不是义务，而是奖励，这是我们这一代人的认知。微量的压力能将人生的电车难题大幅转向好的方向。饥荒让战争无法发生，环境变化已无法追赶。分散在世界各地的民族正聚集到这座监狱。没有腓特烈二世的幸福世界。非人道的概念有时阻碍进步，语言和习俗的统一与其说是对宗教的压迫，不如说是从根本上失去了对无形之物的信任，加速了物质主义。因此，表面的建筑和服装逐渐转变为一种“穿戴”的感觉。国家消失，中产阶级不复存在，狮鹫与马的交配不存在。河马狮鹫仅作为概念存在。这个时代已不再创作音乐、艺术或娱乐。与此相对，生活必需品的生产工资急剧下降。大多数人认为贩卖创意或无形之物是轻松的生意，制造生活必需品的劳动力减少了。然而，业余的逐利艺术无人购买，毫无影响力，仅增加了翻版文化，即复兴商品，导致贫困层和毒品使用者的增加。
Living in a drunken dream, idling away time. Is it a zoetrope, a dream, or reality? Silver-coated, dazzling screens are arranged in a circular pattern at equal intervals, rotating. A sterile place where sound, smell, and touch yield no sensation, yet a flow of light carries the subtle pulse of a heartbeat. Have I finally detached from reality or life? I ponder, darting through the gaps in the curtains. Perhaps I’ve encountered a compulsive hallucination, my self confined here, fleeing to unreality? In this bizarre situation, I believe I’m still making calm judgments. The bright, white screens, flickering with missing dots, project images where no one else exists but me: a human willingly crucified, a hairy biped emerging from thick fog, a youth in black armor standing in shallow water, an old man in a white coat with a mustache glancing at me after deep thought, a monk meditating with closed eyes, behind whom golden ripples glow. There are also incomprehensible or incoherent projections. Silent, fragmented, poor in quality, they reveal something dark and heavy like a mirror when facing the silver screen, eternally reflected in a vast, chilly chamber. A shiver runs down my spine as I notice a painting projected on one screen, milky white with patches of negative colors.
Cut! The director, with a face reveling in fear and negative risks, whispers into a 925 metallic silver transceiver the size of a pinky’s first joint tucked deep in the ear. Great job, excellent acting! Your movements and details were spot-on; I couldn’t imagine this film without you. We got some fantastic shots today—thank you! Looking forward to tomorrow. Before sunset, in the brick-style abandoned warehouse “Daro” in the east, imbued with a mix of positive and negative vibes. This director, with graying hair and dubious, rusty gold-rimmed octagonal sunglasses, has been working with me for about ten years, hasn’t he? Every meeting, he exudes a shallow, wild charm, wearing cologne that screams he’s only interested in seducing women. Probably a nice bergamot and woody blend, but the proportions are off—isoamyl acetate is too overpowering. Where did you find such a vintage perfume? Decades old, right? I heard you stockpile them, but finding one in such good condition is rare. Honestly, I don’t care, but I asked out of communication and curiosity.
Our generation learned that labor is not a duty but a reward. A touch of stress can steer life’s trolley problem significantly toward a better path. Famine makes war impossible, and environmental changes are beyond catching up. Ethnic groups scattered across the world are gathering in this prison. A happy world without Frederick II. The concept of inhumanity sometimes hinders progress, and the unification of language and customs, rather than oppressing religion, erodes trust in the intangible, accelerating materialism. Thus, surface architecture and clothing have shifted to a sense of “wearing.” Nations vanished, the middle class disappeared, and griffins don’t mate with horses. Hippogriffs exist only as concepts. This era no longer produces music, art, or entertainment. Correspondingly, wages for producing life’s essentials have plummeted. Most people believe selling ideas or intangibles is easy money, reducing the workforce for essential goods. Yet amateur, profit-driven art goes unsold, influences nothing, and only fuels recycled culture—revival products—leading to more poverty and drug use.
